## Free, and we mean it

Kalmio is free for users. There's no trial, no "free for the first plan," no feature gate that quietly clicks shut after week three. The core product — meal planning, fridge tracking, the shopping list, the daily dashboard — stays free for everyone, forever.

If you've spent time in this category you know "free" usually has fine print. Ours doesn't. Here's why.

## The mission needs reach

Hungary has serious public-health problems. A quarter of the country is obese, another third overweight, four in ten adults have hypertension, and we lose about six years of life expectancy to the EU average. None of those numbers move if the tool that helps fix them costs 2900 Ft a month.

Kalmio only matters if it reaches a lot of Hungarian households — including ones where 2900 Ft a month is a real expense. So a paywall on the core experience is incompatible with the goal. We had to find a different revenue model.

## Partners pay for the value users create

When you generate a meal plan in Kalmio and then go shopping, you're a high-intent customer with a precise list of what to buy. That's valuable to grocery retailers — Tesco, Kifli, Wolt, Foodrevo, eventually others. Our business model is partnerships with those retailers: when you shop through your Kalmio list, the partner pays us. You don't.

That alignment matters. We're paid when you actually use the product to do something — not when we trick you into a subscription you forget to cancel.

## Premium exists, but only for what costs us per use

There's a paid tier, and we're being honest about what it is: anything that uses an AI agent to reason about your specific situation, and therefore costs us money every time it runs.

- Tap a meal slot to swap it for another? **Free.** That's a deterministic computation we run on our servers; no per-use cost.
- Type *"I don't like broccoli, suggest something else"* and have an AI agent figure out a substitute that fits your week? **Premium.**
- Photograph your off-plan lunch and have it logged automatically? **Premium.**

The split is principled — deterministic versus agentic. The free version is good on its own, not crippled to push you into paying. Premium adds *capabilities*, never unblocks basics.

## Founding Members

For people who want to back this project early, we're opening a one-time **Founding Member** tier at launch — 19 990 Ft, lifetime access to all premium features, capped at the first 1000 users. It's the simplest way to support the work and lock in the agentic features forever. Details on the landing page when it goes live.

## What we're not

We're not selling your data. We're not running ads. We're not putting "sponsored recipes" into your weekly plan. The retail partnership pays us to be useful, and our incentive is to keep being useful — not to extract attention.

That's the whole story.

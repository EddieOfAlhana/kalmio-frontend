## Most nutrition apps fail in the same way

You've installed them. So have I. MyFitnessPal, Lifesum, Yazio. Two weeks of dutiful logging, then a missed meal, then a missed day, then the app sits unopened until you delete it.

The apps didn't fail because they lacked features. They failed because they outsourced the hardest part to you: the logging.

Kalmio takes a different bet. **The plan IS your food log.** When the plan says you're eating Tuesday's lentil főzelék, the system assumes you ate it. No tap to confirm. No photo. No barcode scan. Eat what's in the plan and your day is tracked — silently, automatically, while you go about your life.

## Negative confirmation, not positive

The shift sounds small. It's not. Every other app in this category demands you *prove* you ate something. Kalmio demands the opposite: tell us only when you *didn't*.

That little inversion changes everything. Instead of dozens of micro-decisions per day ("I should log this — I'll do it later — I forgot"), you get one decision per deviation: "I had a gyros for lunch instead." A single tap, and the rest takes care of itself.

## What about the days you don't follow the plan?

You'll have those. Everyone does. So when you log a deviation, Kalmio re-evaluates the rest of your week and shows you what would change — *"Wednesday's lasagna moves to Friday. You don't need to buy spinach this week."* Accept or decline.

That's the loop. Plan → eat → deviate sometimes → re-plan. No guilt, no pie chart of shame, no streak to break.

## What you do once

You set the goal once: how many calories, what dietary preferences, when you go shopping. From then on, Kalmio handles the cognitive load.

Most people don't fail at eating well because they lack willpower. They fail because deciding what to eat — and whether it fits, and whether they have the ingredients, and whether they can afford it — is exhausting. We take that off your plate.
